01 Studio

A studio born
in Sri Lanka,
built for everywhere.

RedOrbit was founded on a conviction we still hold: that distinctive interactive entertainment can be authored from anywhere, and that the next generation of memorable game studios will not all share a postal code.

02 Story

The long version.

RedOrbit Studios began with a handful of engineers, artists, and designers who had spent careers contributing to other people's titles — and decided it was time to author our own. We took our name from the idea of a deliberate trajectory: an orbit isn't drift, it's discipline under gravity.

From a single room in Colombo we've grown into a multidisciplinary studio shipping original IP, partnering with publishers across three continents, and quietly building the kind of pipeline that AAA work demands. We hire for taste as carefully as for talent, and we expect both.

We are not here to make derivative work. We are here to ship games that change someone's relationship to the medium — and to demonstrate, with every release, that world-class can be built from anywhere.

05 Process

How we work,
plainly stated.

Our process is not a secret weapon. It's the inherited wisdom of three decades of game development, applied with discipline and adjusted to what the title actually needs.

STEP / 01

Start with the world

Before mechanics, before milestones, we agree on what the world feels like and what the player leaves with. The creative thesis is the contract every other decision answers to.

STEP / 02

Prototype the unknown

The risky systems get built first. We refuse to defer the hardest questions to the back of the schedule — that's where projects go to die.

STEP / 03

Build playable Fridays

Every Friday, every build, end-to-end playable. It is the single most disciplined thing we do, and it pays back every week.

STEP / 04

Critique without softening

We give each other the feedback the work needs, not the feedback that protects feelings. Kindness is in the standard, not the silence.

STEP / 05

Ship, then iterate

Launch is a milestone, not a finish line. We hold capacity for the first six months because that is where most of the player's relationship with a game is decided.

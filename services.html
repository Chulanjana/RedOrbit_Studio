02 Disciplines

Nine practices.
One studio standard.

01

Full-Cycle Game Development

Concept to launch and beyond. Pre-production, vertical slice, alpha, beta, certification, and post-launch ops — under one production umbrella.

02

Mobile Game Development

iOS and Android titles built to perform on mid-tier hardware. Lifecycle metrics, monetisation hygiene, and performance budgets that hold.

03

PC Game Development

Native Windows, Steam Deck, and storefront-agnostic builds. Mod support, configurability, and the long tail of community love.

04

Unreal Engine 5

Nanite, Lumen, Niagara, and the full UE5 toolchain. Blueprint-and-C++ teams with shipping titles behind them.

05

Unity Development

HDRP and URP pipelines, DOTS where it pays its rent, and the cross-platform reach Unity does best. Mobile to console.

06

3D Environment Art

Worldbuilding from concept to in-engine. Modular kits, terrain pipelines, lighting and atmospheric work that carries narrative weight.

07

Character Design

Hero and crowd characters — concept, sculpt, retopology, rigging, and skinning. PBR materials, look-dev, and motion-capture cleanup.

08

Game Prototyping

Two-week sprints to six-week vertical slices. We answer hard mechanical questions early and produce something a green-lighter can actually play.

09

VR / AR Experiences

Spatial UX, locomotion that doesn't nauseate, and platform-tuned builds for Quest, Vision Pro, and the headsets coming next.

10

Educational & Training Sims

Serious games for aerospace, healthcare, defense, and energy. Real-time fidelity with measurable outcomes — not edutainment.

11

Game UI / UX

HUDs, menus, accessibility, and in-game systems. Interfaces that step aside for play instead of competing with it.

12

Technical R&D

Rendering research, tooling, custom shaders, and engine extensions. The work that doesn't go in marketing material but defines the ceiling.

03 Engagement

How a project moves
through the studio.

Every engagement starts with a clear scope, a named producer, and a milestone schedule that survives contact with reality.

PHASE / 01

Discovery

A two-week paid discovery. We pressure-test the creative brief, surface unknowns, and produce a delivery plan. If we aren't the right studio, this is where we'll say so.

PHASE / 02

Pre-production

Art bible, technical design document, vertical slice scoping, and the architectural decisions that will hold the build together for the next eighteen months.

PHASE / 03

Production

Cross-discipline strike teams under a named producer. Two-week sprints, monthly partner reviews, and a build that is playable every Friday — not just at milestone gates.

PHASE / 04

Polish & Certification

Platform certification, accessibility audits, localisation QA, and the unglamorous month that decides what a launch actually feels like.

PHASE / 05

Launch & Live Ops

Day-one operations and the first six months. Patch cadence, telemetry-led iteration, and community response. The launch is the beginning, not the goal.
